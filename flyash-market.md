<p><p>Flyash, also known as pulverized fuel ash, is a fine ash residue generated from coal-fired power plants and is typically obtained during the combustion process. It consists of small, spherical particles that are rich in mineral content, mainly comprising silicon, aluminum, and calcium. Flyash possesses pozzolanic properties, enabling it to react with water and form cementitious compounds when mixed with lime, making it an essential ingredient in the production of cement, concrete, and other construction materials.</p><p>The Flyash Market has experienced significant growth in recent years due to the increasing demand for sustainable construction materials. <p><p>Fly ash is a byproduct of coal combustion and is used in various markets based on its chemical composition. Class F fly ash, low in lime (CaO) content, is primarily used in concrete applications, improving durability and workability. On the other hand, Class C fly ash, high in lime content, exhibits cementitious properties and is suitable for use in both concrete and cement applications. These market types distinguish between the specific chemical characteristics of fly ash and determine their appropriate usage in construction and engineering industries.</p></p>
