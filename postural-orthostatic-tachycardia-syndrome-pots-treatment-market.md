<p><p>Postural Orthostatic Tachycardia Syndrome (POTS) is a disorder characterized by an abnormal increase in heart rate upon standing up, causing symptoms like lightheadedness, fainting, and fatigue. Treatment for POTS aims to manage symptoms and improve the patient's quality of life.</p><p>The treatment for POTS typically involves a multidisciplinary approach that includes lifestyle modifications, medication, and physical therapy. Lifestyle modifications may include increasing fluid and salt intake, wearing compression stockings, and engaging in regular exercise. Medications like beta blockers, fludrocortisone, and midodrine may be prescribed to help manage symptoms and regulate heart rate.
