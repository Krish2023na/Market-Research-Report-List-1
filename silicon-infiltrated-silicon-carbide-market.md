<p><p>Silicon-infiltrated silicon carbide (SiSiC) is a composite material formed by infiltrating molten silicon into a porous carbon matrix made of silicon carbide (SiC). This process creates a dense and strong material with excellent thermal conductivity, wear resistance, and mechanical properties. SiSiC is widely used in various industries, including electronics, automotive, aerospace, defense, and energy, due to its superior performance characteristics.</p><p>The market analysis of the silicon-infiltrated silicon carbide market indicates a steady growth rate during the forecast period. The increasing demand for SiSiC in high-temperature applications, such as furnace components, sensors, and heat exchangers, is driving market growth. The material's ability to withstand extreme environments and its excellent thermal, electrical, and chemical properties make it highly desirable in these applications.</p><p>Moreover, the growing focus on lightweight and fuel-efficient vehicles is fueling the demand for SiSiC in the automotive industry.
