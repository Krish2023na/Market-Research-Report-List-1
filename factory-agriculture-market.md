<p><p>Factory agriculture, also known as industrial agriculture or agribusiness, refers to the mass production of crops and livestock using modern technological methods and machinery. It involves large-scale operations that aim for high productivity and efficiency.</p><p>Currently, factory agriculture dominates the global food production system, supplying the majority of the world's food. This market is driven by the increasing demand for food due to the growing population, urbanization, and changing dietary preferences. Additionally, the need for higher yield and faster production to meet this demand have fueled the growth of factory agriculture.</p><p>However, there are concerns and criticisms associated with factory agriculture. It is often criticized for its negative impact on the environment, including deforestation, water pollution, greenhouse gas emissions, and loss of biodiversity. Moreover, the widespread use of agrochemicals and antibiotics has raised concerns about food safety and public health.</p><p>The future outlook of the factory agriculture market is likely to face some challenges. There is an increasing emphasis on sustainable and environmentally-friendly agricultural practices, which may push for a shift away from large-scale industrial operations. Consumer demand for organic and locally-sourced food is also growing, indicating a potential market for alternative agriculture methods.</p><p>Furthermore, the rise of technology and innovation in agriculture, such as vertical farming, hydroponics, and precision farming, may disrupt the traditional factory agriculture market.
