<p><p>Aldehyde Ketone Resins are divided into two market types: Photopolymerization and Thermal Polymerization. </p><p>Photopolymerization refers to the process of curing these resins using ultraviolet (UV) or visible light. This is achieved by adding a photoinitiator, which triggers a chemical reaction that leads to the formation of a solid and durable film.</p><p>On the other hand, Thermal Polymerization involves the curing of Aldehyde Ketone Resins through the application of heat. This process activates the resin's cross-linking agents and initiates a chemical reaction that results in the formation of a strong and heat-resistant film.</p><p>Both market types offer unique advantages and applications, catering to different industry requirements for adhesives, coatings, and composite materials.</p></p>
