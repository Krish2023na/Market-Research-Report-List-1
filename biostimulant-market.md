This trend is primarily due to the growing consumer preference for organic food and the stringent regulations on the use of chemical fertilizers and pesticides. Biostimulants offer an eco-friendly and sustainable approach to enhance crop productivity without harming the environment.</p><p>Another emerging trend is the integration of biostimulants into precision agriculture practices. Precision agriculture utilizes advanced technologies such as remote sensing, GPS, and artificial intelligence to optimize farm practices. Biostimulants can be used in conjunction with precision agriculture techniques to enhance nutrient management, improve soil health, and achieve better crop performance.</p><p>Furthermore, there is a growing focus on research and development activities to develop innovative and effective biostimulant products. <p><p>The biostimulant market can be categorized into different types based on their composition and mode of action. Acid-based biostimulants are derived from acids such as humic acid and fulvic acid, which enhance nutrient availability and plant growth. Extract-based biostimulants are obtained from natural extracts like seaweed, algae, and plant extracts, which promote hormonal and metabolic activities in plants. Other types include microbial-based biostimulants, which contain beneficial microorganisms that improve soil fertility and plant health. These different types offer various benefits and are used in agriculture to improve crop yield and quality.</p></p>
