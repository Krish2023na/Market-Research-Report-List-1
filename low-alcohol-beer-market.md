<p><p>Low-alcohol beer refers to a type of beer that contains a lower alcohol content than traditional beers. There are two main types of low-alcohol beer market: Limit Fermentation and Dealcoholization Method. Limit Fermentation involves controlling yeast activity during fermentation to limit alcohol production. This method results in a naturally low-alcohol beer. On the other hand, the Dealcoholization Method involves removing alcohol from traditionally brewed beer through techniques like vacuum distillation or reverse osmosis. This method allows for the production of zero-alcohol or low-alcohol beer variants.</p></p>
