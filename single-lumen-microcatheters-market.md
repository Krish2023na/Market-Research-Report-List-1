They are commonly used in minimally invasive procedures to ensure precise and targeted drug delivery.</p><p>Diagnosis microcatheters aid in the diagnosis of diseases or conditions by providing access to difficult-to-reach areas of the body. They enable healthcare professionals to obtain tissue samples or perform imaging procedures to gather accurate diagnostic information.</p><p>Aspiration microcatheters are used to remove fluids or debris from specific body locations. They are frequently employed in procedures such as the removal of blood clots or excess fluid from organs.</p><p>Steerable microcatheters possess the ability to navigate through complex anatomical structures or blood vessels. They offer enhanced maneuverability, making them valuable for procedures that require precise control and accessibility in challenging anatomical locations.</p></p>
