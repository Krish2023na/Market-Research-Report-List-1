<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Automotive Torque Vectoring Systems refer to technologies that distribute torque to individual wheels in order to improve vehicle performance and handling. Active Torque Vectoring Systems (ATVS) actively adjust the torque distribution based on various factors like steering input and road conditions, providing precise and dynamic control. On the other hand, Passive Torque Vectoring Systems (PTVS) rely on mechanical components like differentials or limited-slip differentials to distribute torque based on wheel speed and traction, offering a more predictable and stable handling experience.</p></p>
