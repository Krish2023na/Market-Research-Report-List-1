<p><p>Lower extremity bone fixation screws are medical devices used in orthopedic surgeries to stabilize and fix fractures in the bones of the lower limbs. These screws are available in three types: stainless-steel, titanium, and bioabsorbable. Stainless-steel screws are strong and resistant to corrosion, while titanium screws offer superior strength, biocompatibility, and reduced artifact in imaging. On the other hand, bioabsorbable screws are made from materials that can gradually dissolve over time, reducing the need for removal surgeries and allowing for natural bone healing.</p></p>
