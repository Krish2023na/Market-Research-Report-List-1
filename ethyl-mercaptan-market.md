<p><ul><li>Ethanol (or Ethylene) Method</li><li>Ethyl Chloride Method</li><li>Anhydrous Ethanol Method</li><li>Other Method</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The ethyl mercaptan market can be categorized into four types based on the production methods. The first is the Ethanol (or Ethylene) Method, which involves reacting ethanol (or ethylene) with hydrogen sulfide to produce ethyl mercaptan. The second is the Ethyl Chloride Method, where hydrogen sulfide reacts with ethyl chloride to form the product. The third is the Anhydrous Ethanol Method, which combines anhydrous ethanol with hydrogen sulfide to produce ethyl mercaptan. Lastly, the Other Method encompasses alternative approaches used to manufacture ethyl mercaptan apart from the aforementioned methods.</p></p>
