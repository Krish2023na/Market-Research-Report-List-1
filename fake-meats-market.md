<p><p>The fake meats market encompasses various types including tofu-based, tempeh-based, TVP-based, seitan-based, Quorn-based, and others. Tofu-based fake meats are made from soybean curds, while tempeh-based products are derived from fermented soybeans. TVP-based options are typically made from defatted soy flour, and seitan-based products are mainly composed of wheat gluten. Quorn-based alternatives are derived from mycoprotein, a type of fungus. Other types of fake meats may include products made from legumes, grains, or even genetically modified organisms. These alternatives aim to mimic the taste, texture, and nutritional profile of traditional animal-based meats.</p></p>
