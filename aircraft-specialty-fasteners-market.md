<p><ul><li>Threaded Fasteners</li><li>Non-Threaded Fasteners</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The aircraft specialty fasteners market consists of two main types: threaded fasteners and non-threaded fasteners. Threaded fasteners refer to screws, bolts, and nuts that have external or internal threads, allowing them to be secured or detached by twisting. They provide a strong and reliable connection in aircraft structures. On the other hand, non-threaded fasteners include rivets, pins, and clips that do not have threads. Instead, they rely on their shape or design to secure components together, offering a more lightweight and efficient solution for certain aircraft applications.</p></p>
