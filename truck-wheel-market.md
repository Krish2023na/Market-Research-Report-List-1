<p><p>The truck wheel market can be categorized into three types: casting, forging, and other markets. Casting involves pouring molten metal into a mold and allowing it to solidify, resulting in wheels that are durable, cost-effective, and suitable for standard trucking needs. Forging, on the other hand, involves shaping heated metal through compression, resulting in stronger and more durable wheels ideal for heavy-duty trucks or rough terrains. The "other market" category includes alternative wheel manufacturing methods, such as machining or additive manufacturing, which offer unique advantages or cater to niche market demands.</p></p>
