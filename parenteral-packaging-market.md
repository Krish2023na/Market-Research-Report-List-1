<p><p>The parenteral packaging market includes various types of packaging for medications that are administered through injection or infusion. Bags are flexible containers used for large volumes of liquid medications. Ampoules are small sealed glass containers for single-dose injections. Vials are glass or plastic containers for multiple-dose medications. Prefilled syringes and cartridges are pre-filled with medication for easy administration. Ready-to-mix systems provide two or more components that are mixed together before injection. All of these packaging types help ensure the safety, sterility, and accurate delivery of medications.</p></p>
