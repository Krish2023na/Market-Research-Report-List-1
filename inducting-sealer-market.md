<p><p>An inducting sealer, also known as an induction sealing machine, is a device used to create a hermetic seal on containers. It works by utilizing electromagnetic induction to heat a metallic foil or liner, which then melts onto the container's rim, creating a tight seal. Inducting sealers are commonly used in various industries such as pharmaceuticals, food and beverage, cosmetics, and chemicals, as they provide a tamper-evident and leak-proof sealing solution.</p><p>The future outlook for the inducting sealer market is positive, with a projected growth rate of 6.4% during the forecasted period.
