<p><p>Refractory Clay is categorized into two types: Hard Clay and Soft Clay. Hard Clay refers to a type of refractory clay that has high density, low plasticity, and excellent resistance to heat and chemical erosion. It is commonly used in the manufacturing of refractory bricks and crucibles. On the other hand, Soft Clay is a type of refractory clay with low density and high plasticity. It is mainly used in industries where malleability is required, such as in the production of pottery and ceramics. Both types of clay cater to different industrial needs based on their distinct physical properties.</p></p>
