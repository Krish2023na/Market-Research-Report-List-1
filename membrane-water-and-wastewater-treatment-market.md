<p><p>The membrane water and wastewater treatment market includes various types of filtration technologies: microfiltration, ultrafiltration, nanofiltration, and reverse osmosis. Microfiltration uses a porous membrane to remove larger particles and impurities. Ultrafiltration employs a finer membrane that eliminates smaller contaminants like bacteria and viruses. Nanofiltration filters even smaller molecules while allowing some dissolved ions to pass through. Reverse osmosis is the most advanced filtration technique where water is forced through a semipermeable membrane, removing nearly all impurities. These technologies are crucial in ensuring clean and safe drinking water as well as treating wastewater for reuse.</p></p>
