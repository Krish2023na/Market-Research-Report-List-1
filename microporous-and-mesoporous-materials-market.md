<p><ul><li>Microporous Materials (Less than 2 nm)</li><li>Mesoporous Materials (2-50 nm)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Microporous materials refer to a type of substance with pore sizes less than 2 nm. These materials possess a high surface area and are commonly used in applications such as molecular sieves and gas separation. On the other hand, mesoporous materials have pore sizes ranging from 2-50 nm. They have a larger pore size compared to microporous materials, allowing for the efficient absorption and filtration of larger molecules. These mesoporous materials find applications in areas such as catalysis, adsorbents, and drug delivery systems.</p></p>
