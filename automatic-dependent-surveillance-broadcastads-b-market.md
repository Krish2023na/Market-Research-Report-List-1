<p><p>Automatic Dependent Surveillance Broadcast (ADS-B) is a technology used in aviation for improved aircraft tracking and situational awareness. ADS-B has three market types: ADS-B Out, ADS-B In, and ADS-B Ground Stations. ADS-B Out refers to the transmission of an aircraft's precise position, velocity, and other flight information to ground-based receivers, enhancing aircraft visibility. ADS-B In enables aircraft to receive and display information from other equipped aircraft, providing real-time traffic awareness. ADS-B Ground Stations receive and process information from ADS-B Out equipped aircraft, enabling air traffic controllers to monitor and manage airspace more efficiently.</p></p>
