<p><p>The ethylene-chlorotrifluoroethylene copolymer market is categorized into different types based on the polymerization method used. Emulsion polymerization involves the mixing of monomers in water to form a stable emulsion. Suspension polymerization utilizes a non-water solvent to suspend monomers, leading to the formation of polymer particles. The post emulsification method involves creating a pre-emulsion followed by emulsifying it with a surfactant. Shear emulsification method involves using high shear forces to disperse the monomers and create an emulsion.</p></p>
