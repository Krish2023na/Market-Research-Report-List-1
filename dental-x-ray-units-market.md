<p><p>Dental X-ray units are used in dentistry to capture images of teeth and surrounding structures. There are two main types of these units: intraoral and extraoral. Intraoral X-ray units are used to take images inside the mouth, providing detailed views of individual teeth and their roots. On the other hand, extraoral X-ray units capture images outside the mouth, offering a broader view of the entire jaw and surrounding structures. Both types of units play a crucial role in diagnosing dental conditions and planning treatments.</p></p>
