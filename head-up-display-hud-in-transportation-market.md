<p><p>Head-Up Display (HUD) refers to a technology used in transportation that projects essential information directly onto the vehicle's windshield. There are two types of HUDs available in the market. The first type, known as windshield-projected HUDs, uses a display source to project information onto the windshield, allowing drivers to view it without shifting their gaze. The second type, called combiner-projected HUDs, uses a transparent screen placed in front of the driver, reflecting information from the display source. Both types of HUDs enhance driver safety by providing crucial information within their line of sight.</p></p>
