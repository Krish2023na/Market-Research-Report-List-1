<p><p>Inks for planographic printing, also known as lithographic inks, are formulated specifically for lithographic or offset printing processes. These inks are commonly used in commercial printing, packaging, and publishing industries. Planographic printing involves the transfer of ink from a printing plate onto a rubber blanket, which then transfers the image onto the printing substrate.</p><p>The demand for inks for planographic printing is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. One of the key drivers is the increasing demand for packaging materials, fueled by the growth of e-commerce and the need for attractive packaging to enhance product appeal. Additionally, the rise in advertising and promotional activities, especially in emerging economies, is driving the demand for planographic printing inks. The ability of these inks to offer high-quality, high-speed printing with low waste further boosts their adoption.</p><p>Increasing environmental concerns and regulations regarding ink formulations have also led to the development of eco-friendly inks for planographic printing. Manufacturers are focusing on producing inks with low VOC (volatile organic compound) content and improved sustainability features, while still maintaining excellent print quality. This shift towards eco-friendly inks is likely to propel the market growth further.</p><p>The future outlook of the inks for planographic printing market appears promising. The market is expected to benefit from technological advancements and research and development efforts aimed at producing inks with improved properties, such as faster drying time, better adhesion, and enhanced color saturation. Additionally, the growing popularity of digital printing techniques may pose a challenge to the market, but planographic printing continues to dominate for large-scale, high-speed commercial printing.</p><p>According to the provided information, the market is projected to grow at a CAGR of 13.3% during the forecasted period. <p><p>In the market of inks for planographic printing, there are three main types available: water-based inks, solvent-based inks, and UV-cured inks. Water-based inks utilize water as their main carrier, making them environmentally friendly and easy to clean up. Solvent-based inks, on the other hand, use organic solvents as their carrier, providing excellent adhesion and color intensity but may release harmful volatile organic compounds. UV-cured inks are dried instantly using ultraviolet light, resulting in high print quality, fast production, and minimal environmental impact.</p></p>
