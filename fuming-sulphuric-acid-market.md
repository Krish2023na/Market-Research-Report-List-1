It is produced in different concentrations, denoted by H2S2O7, H4S2O8, and H6S3O12. These concentrations represent the amount of sulfur trioxide (SO3) dissolved in sulfuric acid (H2SO4). H2S2O7 has 20-30% SO3, H4S2O8 has 40-50% SO3, and H6S3O12 has 60-70% SO3. The different concentrations of fuming sulphuric acid allow for versatility in its applications, including acid catalysts, sulfonating agents, and dehydrating agents in chemical processes and manufacturing.</p></p>
