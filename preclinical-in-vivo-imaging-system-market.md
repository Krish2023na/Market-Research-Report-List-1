<p><p>The Preclinical In-vivo Imaging System market comprises different types of imaging systems that are used in preclinical research and drug development. Optical Imaging Systems use light to create images of biological structures and processes. Nuclear Imaging Systems use radioactive substances to detect and track molecules in living organisms. Micro MRI Systems use strong magnets and radio waves to generate detailed images of small animals. Micro CT Systems use X-rays to produce 3D images of small animals. Micro Ultrasound Systems use high-frequency sound waves to create images of small animals. Photoacoustic and Magnetic Particle Imaging Systems use laser or magnetic fields to create detailed images of biological structures and functions.</p></p>
