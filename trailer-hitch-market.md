<p><p>Trailer hitches are categorized into five classes based on their towing capacities. Class I is designed for light-duty applications, handling loads up to 2,000 pounds. Class II is suited for small trailers and provides a capacity of up to 3,500 pounds. Class III hitches are commonly used for mid-sized trailers and can tow loads up to 6,000 pounds. Class IV hitches are heavy-duty, capable of towing up to 10,000 pounds. Lastly, Class V hitches are the strongest, enabling towing capacities exceeding 10,000 pounds, making them ideal for large trailers or RVs.</p></p>
