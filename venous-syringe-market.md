<p><p>The venous syringe market consists of two main types: disposable and non-disposable syringes. Disposable syringes are designed for single use and are disposed of after each use to prevent the spread of infections. They offer convenience, safety, and cost-effectiveness for healthcare settings. On the other hand, non-disposable syringes are designed to be reused multiple times after proper sterilization. They are usually made of high-quality materials and are preferred in certain clinical settings where sterilization processes are readily available. The choice between disposable and non-disposable syringes depends on factors such as cost, convenience, and infection control practices.</p></p>
