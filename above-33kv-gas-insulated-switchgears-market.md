<p><p>Above 33KV Gas Insulated Switchgears are a type of electrical devices used to control, protect, and regulate the flow of electricity in high voltage power systems. They are categorized into two types: Primary Gas Insulated Switchgear (GIS) and Secondary Gas Insulated Switchgear. </p><p>Primary GIS is connected directly to the high voltage network and is responsible for receiving and distributing electricity. It is designed to withstand high voltage levels and offers reliable performance.</p><p>Secondary GIS is installed downstream from the primary GIS and is responsible for further distribution to end-users. It ensures the safe and efficient delivery of electricity to consumers.</p></p>
