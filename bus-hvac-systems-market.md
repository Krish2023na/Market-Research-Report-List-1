<p><p>Bus HVAC systems refer to the heating, ventilation, and air conditioning systems used in buses. There are two main types of bus HVAC systems: engine-powered HVAC systems and electric-powered HVAC systems. Engine-powered systems are driven by the bus engine, using its power to provide heating and cooling inside the bus. On the other hand, electric-powered systems use electricity from external sources to operate and regulate the temperature inside the bus. Both types serve the same purpose of creating a comfortable environment for passengers, but they differ in terms of power source and operation.</p></p>
