<p><p>Bleached absorbent cotton refers to the cotton fibers that have undergone a bleaching process to increase their absorbency. There are two main market types of bleached absorbent cotton: virgin cotton and noil.</p><p>Virgin cotton is a type of cotton that is primarily used in the production of high-quality absorbent cotton products. It is made from the long and smooth cotton fibers obtained directly from the cotton plant. Virgin cotton is considered to be the purest and most premium form of cotton, resulting in superior absorbency and softness.</p><p>Noil refers to the shorter and coarser fibers that are obtained during the cotton processing. These fibers are generally considered to be of lower quality compared to virgin cotton. However, they still have some absorbent properties and are used in the production of lower-grade absorbent cotton products.</p><p>In summary, the bleached absorbent cotton market consists of two main types: virgin cotton, which is high-quality cotton fibers directly obtained from the cotton plant, and noil, which refers to the shorter and coarser cotton fibers resulting from the cotton processing.</p></p>
