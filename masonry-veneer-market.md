<p><strong>The Masonry Veneer Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Adhered Veneer</li><li>Anchored Veneer</li><li>Articulated Veneer</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Masonry veneer is a popular choice for exterior cladding, and it can be categorized into various types. Adhered veneer involves applying a thin layer of masonry directly onto the building's surface using mortar or adhesive. Anchored veneer involves attaching the veneer to the building by mechanical anchoring systems, providing greater stability. Articulated veneer combines adhered and anchored techniques, employing both adhesive and mechanical reinforcement. Other types may include prefabricated panel systems or thin stone veneers. These different market types offer versatility in design, durability, and installation methods for masonry veneer applications.</p></p>
