<p><p>Ceramic Matrix Composites (CMCs) are advanced materials that offer superior mechanical and thermal properties compared to traditional ceramics. The market for CMCs can be categorized into different types based on the composition of the matrix material. Oxide-based CMCs utilize ceramic oxides like alumina, zirconia, and spinel as the matrix material. Silicon carbide-based CMCs employ silicon carbide as the matrix material and are known for their excellent heat resistance. Carbon-based CMCs use carbon as the matrix material, offering lightweight and high mechanical strength. Other types of CMCs may incorporate different ceramic materials in the matrix.</p></p>
