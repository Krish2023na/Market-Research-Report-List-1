<p><p>The nickel wire market is divided into two main types: nickel wire and nickel alloy wire. Nickel wire refers to the pure form of nickel, characterized by its high melting point, corrosion resistance, and electrical conductivity. It is commonly used in applications where these properties are essential, such as in the production of heating elements and electrical wires. On the other hand, nickel alloy wire is a combination of nickel with other metals, like chromium or iron, to enhance certain properties like high temperature resistance and strength. This type of wire finds applications in industries such as aerospace, automotive, and electronics.</p></p>
