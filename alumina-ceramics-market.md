<p><p>Alumina ceramics is classified into two main types: high purity type and normal type. The high purity type is composed of ceramics with a significantly high purity level, with impurity concentrations kept at a minimum. This type is generally used in applications that require excellent electrical insulation properties and high-temperature resistance. On the other hand, the normal type refers to ceramics with a lower purity level and is commonly used in a wider range of applications, such as structural components, cutting tools, and electronic substrates, where extreme purity is not necessary.</p></p>
