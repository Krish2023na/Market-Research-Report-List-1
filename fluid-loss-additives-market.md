There are three main types of fluid loss additives: synthetically modified natural additives, synthetic additives, and natural additives. </p><p>Synthetically modified natural additives, such as polyanionic cellulose (PAC) and carboxymethyl cellulose (CMC), are derived from natural materials but treated chemically to enhance their properties. Synthetic additives, like acrylic sulphonated polymers, are produced artificially and can provide better fluid loss control. Natural additives, such as starch, lignin, and bentonite, are derived from naturally occurring materials without any chemical modification. They offer decent fluid loss control properties but might be less effective compared to synthetic alternatives.</p></p>
